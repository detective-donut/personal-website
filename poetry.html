My Poems

Unreal

It felt unreal.
The lights, the letters, the red-inked seal.
Twenty thousand steps and one deep breath,
All blurred into that singular feel.

Written the day after walking 20,202 steps. The day it started to feel real.

Poems I Read

Thoughts on pieces I’ve been haunted by.

"The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock" – T.S. Eliot

Prufrock’s paralysis feels contemporary — the fear of speaking, of acting, of being misunderstood. What strikes me is how Eliot layers anxiety with aesthetics. The poem doesn’t scream. It simmers.
