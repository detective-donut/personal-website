Hi, I’m Amal Zahra.

I’m a CS undergrad at Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar and a girl who once topped the world in O Level Math. Since then, I’ve co-authored a research piece in the Harvard Business Review exploring AI and leadership, while creating data structures for surreal numbers with LUMS faculty. I founded Qafla to teach girls how to code in my ancestral village in Chakwal. Currently, I serve as one of the youngest members of the New York Academy of Sciences.

At heart, I’m a mathematician and a poet who loves to think. Here, I write my thoughts.
